How to choose a hotel (fast)

Decide in the right order to avoid wasting time.

  1. Pick the neighborhood: prioritize transit + walkability over “nice rooms”.
  2. Set your non-negotiables: A/C (summer), elevator, quiet, breakfast, 24h desk.
  3. Define your trip style: early sightseeing vs nightlife vs business.
  4. Shortlist 3–5 options: compare total cost, not just nightly rate.
  5. Lock cancellation rules: choose flexible if your trip might change.
Quick rule: If you’re taking transit daily, a “better location” beats a “better hotel” almost every time.

Booking tips (fees, cancellations, and traps)

Hotels can look cheap until add-ons appear.

  • Total price: confirm taxes, city fees, resort fees, and breakfast costs.
  • Room type details: “small double” can be very small; check square meters/ft² when available.
  • Cancellation: flexible rates are worth it when plans might shift.
  • Noise: ask for courtyard-facing rooms if you sleep lightly.
  • Air conditioning: confirm it explicitly in warm months.
  • Late check-in: make sure your arrival time is supported.
Decision shortcut: After you shortlist 3 options, stop browsing and book the best location with the best cancellation policy you can afford.

FAQ

Is it better to stay in the city center?

Often yes for first-time visitors, because it reduces daily transit time. If you’re on a tighter budget, stay near a major transit line instead of far suburbs.

What matters most: hotel quality or location?

Location usually wins. A good location saves time and stress, and it makes the whole trip feel easier.

What are the most common hidden costs?

City taxes, resort fees (in some destinations), breakfast add-ons, and paid early check-in/late check-out are common extras.

How many hotels should I shortlist?

Three to five. More than that usually creates decision fatigue and doesn’t improve outcomes.
