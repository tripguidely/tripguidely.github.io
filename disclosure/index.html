Affiliate Disclosure

Last updated: 2026-02-27

TripGuidely participates in affiliate marketing programs. This means that some of the links on this website are affiliate links.

What does this mean?

If you click on an affiliate link and make a booking or purchase, we may earn a small commission. This comes at no additional cost to you.

Why we use affiliate links

Affiliate commissions help support the operation of this website, including research, writing, editing, and maintaining up-to-date travel guides.

Our goal is to provide helpful, structured travel information that allows you to make informed decisions. Affiliate partnerships allow us to continue publishing free content.

Editorial independence

Our recommendations are based on independent research, structured comparison, and practical decision-making criteria.

Compensation does not influence our editorial judgments. We aim to present clear trade-offs, pros and cons, and relevant planning considerations.

Affiliate programs

TripGuidely may participate in travel affiliate programs including, but not limited to:

  • Travel booking platforms
  • Hotel reservation networks
  • Cruise booking partners
  • Car rental platforms

Specific partners may change over time as we refine booking options and improve the user experience.

Your trust matters

Transparency is important to us. If you have questions about our recommendations or affiliate relationships, please contact us.
