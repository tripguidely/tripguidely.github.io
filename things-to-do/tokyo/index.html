Build your Tokyo plan in 60 seconds

Pick your trip style, then follow the playbook.

If you have 2 days

  • Day 1: Asakusa + Ueno (temples, parks, old Tokyo).
  • Day 2: Shibuya + Shinjuku (city energy + viewpoints).
  • One timed anchor: observation deck or a themed experience.

If you want day trips

  • Pick one: Nikko / Hakone / Kamakura.
  • Treat it as an anchor day: don’t stack another heavy-ticket day after.
  • Keep Tokyo evening light: food streets + viewpoints.
One rule to remember: Tokyo punishes zig-zags. Keep your pins in one district, then move like a chess knight, not a ping-pong ball.

Booking strategy (how to keep Tokyo smooth)

Not everything needs a ticket, but the right anchors prevent chaos.

  • Anchor 1–2 timed items: observation deck, themed experience, or a day trip.
  • Plan by district: one main area per half-day (less transit, more doing).
  • Keep buffers: stations are big and transfers take time.
  • Weather logic: save indoor (museums/arcades) as your plan B.

District clusters (Tokyo done right)

Choose one cluster per half-day and your itinerary instantly feels easier.

Asakusa + Ueno (classic + calm)

  • Anchor option: early Senso-ji
  • Fill: Ueno park + nearby museums + food streets
  • Best for: first morning, jet lag friendly

Shibuya + Harajuku (energy + shopping)

  • Anchor option: sunset viewpoint nearby
  • Fill: streets, cafés, easy wandering
  • Best for: evening vibes

Shinjuku (night city + viewpoints)

  • Anchor: observation deck (sunset)
  • Fill: food alleys, city lights
  • Best for: “Tokyo at night” feeling

Akihabara (tech + pop culture)

  • Best time: afternoon
  • Fill: arcades, themed shops, cafés
  • Best for: indoor-friendly day

2-day Tokyo itinerary framework

Two tight days, built for district flow.

Day 1: Asakusa + Ueno + Akihabara

  • Morning: Asakusa (Senso-ji) early
  • Midday: Ueno park + museums (optional)
  • Afternoon: Akihabara (indoor-friendly)
  • Evening: relaxed food street nearby

Day 2: Shibuya + Harajuku + Shinjuku

  • Morning: Harajuku / parks (easy start)
  • Afternoon: Shibuya streets
  • Sunset: observation deck (anchor)
  • Night: Shinjuku food alleys + lights

FAQ

Quick answers before you book.

How many days do you need in Tokyo?

3 days is a great baseline. 2 days works if you cluster districts and keep day trips optional.

Do I need to book attractions in advance?

Book 1–2 timed anchors (deck/day trip/themed experience). Most neighborhoods and temples can stay flexible.

How do I avoid wasting time on transit?

Plan by district: one main area per half-day. Avoid cross-city zig-zagging at midday.
