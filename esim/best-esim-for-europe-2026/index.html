Setup: install an eSIM in minutes

Do this at home on Wi-Fi so your airport brain can stay peaceful.

  1. Check compatibility: confirm your phone supports eSIM.
  2. Buy your plan: choose Europe regional or a country plan.
  3. Install the eSIM: follow the app instructions (QR/manual).
  4. Set eSIM as data line: keep your primary SIM for calls/texts.
  5. Turn off roaming on your primary SIM: avoids accidental charges.
  6. Test before you fly: ensure the eSIM is installed and ready.
Common mistake: leaving data roaming enabled on your main SIM. Disable it, then use the eSIM for data.

Best practices for trouble-free data

Small settings, big difference.

  • Download offline maps: Google Maps offline areas as backup.
  • Use WhatsApp/iMessage: keep calls/texts data-based when possible.
  • Low Data Mode: reduce background app usage.
  • Hotspot carefully: tethering can burn data fast.

FAQ

What is the best eSIM for Europe in 2026?

For most travelers, Airalo is a strong pick because you can buy a plan online, install quickly, and use data across many European countries without needing a physical SIM.

Will an eSIM work in multiple European countries?

Yes. Choose a regional plan that covers multiple countries, or a country plan if your trip is mostly in one destination.

Do I keep my regular SIM active?

Usually yes. Keep your primary SIM for calls/texts if needed, and set the eSIM as the data line. Turn off data roaming on your primary SIM to avoid charges.

Is my phone compatible with eSIM?

Most newer iPhones and many recent Android devices support eSIM. Check your phone settings for an eSIM option or confirm your specific model supports eSIM.

Is an eSIM cheaper than roaming?

In many cases, yes. Roaming passes and pay-per-MB fees add up quickly. A prepaid eSIM plan gives predictable costs.
