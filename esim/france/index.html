Setup: install an eSIM in minutes

Do this at home on Wi-Fi so arrival is calm.

  1. Check compatibility: confirm your phone supports eSIM.
  2. Buy your plan: pick data size based on your trip style.
  3. Install the eSIM: follow the app instructions (QR/manual).
  4. Set eSIM as data line: keep your primary SIM for calls/texts.
  5. Turn off roaming on your primary SIM: avoids accidental charges.
  6. Activate on arrival: turn the eSIM line on when you land.
Common mistake: forgetting to switch the data line to the eSIM. If data doesn’t work, check Cellular settings first.

FAQ

What is the best eSIM for France in 2026?

For most travelers, Airalo is a strong pick because you can buy a plan online, install quickly, and get prepaid data that’s easier to control than roaming.

How much data do I need for France?

Maps + messaging can work with a smaller plan. If you’ll upload photos, stream video, or hotspot, choose a larger plan.

Can I set up the eSIM before landing in France?

Yes. Install it on Wi-Fi before your trip, then activate it when you arrive. That’s the smoothest setup.

Do I keep my regular SIM active?

Usually yes. Keep your primary SIM for calls/texts if needed, and set the eSIM as the data line. Turn off data roaming on your primary SIM to avoid charges.
