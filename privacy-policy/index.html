Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-02-27

This Privacy Policy explains how TripGuidely (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses, and protects information when you visit tripguidely.github.io (the “Site”).

1) Information we collect

We try to collect as little personal information as possible. Depending on how you use the Site, we may collect:

  • Basic usage data (pages visited, approximate location, device/browser, referral source).
  • Interaction data (for example, when you click outbound links).
  • Technical data (IP address may be processed for security/analytics, but we configure analytics to reduce identifiability where possible).

2) Analytics (Google Analytics 4)

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand how visitors use the Site and to improve content and navigation. We configured GA4 to be more privacy-friendly by disabling Google signals and ad personalization signals.

Analytics data may include: pages viewed, scroll/engagement signals, approximate region, device type, and traffic source.

3) Outbound link tracking

To understand which resources are useful, we may measure outbound link clicks (clicks to websites outside TripGuidely). This event can include the destination URL as an event label.

We use this only for performance measurement and content improvement (for example: which booking resources people actually use).

4) Affiliate links

Some links on the Site may be affiliate links. If you click an affiliate link and make a purchase or booking, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.

For more details, see our Affiliate Disclosure.

5) Third-party scripts and partners

The Site may load third-party scripts that support affiliate verification and monetization (for example Travelpayouts tools), as well as analytics scripts (Google tag / GA4).

These third parties may process data according to their own policies. We recommend reviewing their policies if you want more detail:

  • Google (Analytics / Google tag)
  • Travelpayouts (affiliate tools)

6) Cookies

The Site may use cookies or similar technologies from analytics and affiliate partners. Cookies can help measure performance, prevent fraud, and support attribution for affiliate programs.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Blocking cookies may affect certain site features and tracking accuracy.

7) Your choices

  • Browser controls: You can block or delete cookies using your browser.
  • Do Not Track: Some browsers send DNT signals. The Site may not respond consistently to DNT signals.

8) Data retention

We keep analytics data only as long as needed for reporting, trend analysis, and site improvement. Retention and processing may also depend on third-party platform settings.

9) Children’s privacy

The Site is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

10) Security

We take reasonable steps to protect the Site and its data. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

11) Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top of the page will reflect changes.
