Best pickup locations for Paris

Pickups can make or break your first hour. Choose convenience over “perfect”.

  • CDG (Charles de Gaulle): convenient if you land and leave the city soon.
  • ORY (Orly): practical for south/Loire/Versailles routes.
  • Train stations: popular if you arrive by rail and start a road trip right away.
  • Outer Paris: often less stressful than the dense center for pickup and return.
Rule of thumb: If you’re staying in central Paris for a few days, don’t rent until the day you leave the city.

Fees, deposits & common traps

Most “bad experiences” come from one of these.

  • Deposit size: check the hold amount and accepted cards.
  • Fuel policy: prefer “full-to-full” when possible.
  • Extra driver: can add daily fees—confirm before checkout.
  • Young driver: fees often apply below certain ages.
  • Cross-border driving: tell the rental company if you’ll leave France.
  • Tolls: France has toll roads—plan for it in your budget.
Checklist at pickup: photograph the car (all sides + wheels), confirm fuel level, and keep the return receipt.

Driving & parking tips (Paris reality)

Driving in central Paris can be slow and expensive. Plan around it.

  • Use the Metro inside Paris: rent only for the days you leave the city.
  • Parking: prefer paid garages; street parking is limited and regulated.
  • Navigation: download offline maps + keep a charging cable.
  • Rush hours: avoid leaving/entering Paris during peak traffic.

FAQ

Is it worth renting a car in Paris?

For central Paris, a car is usually not worth it due to traffic and parking. Renting is best for day trips and road trips outside the city.

Where should I pick up my rental car?

Most travelers prefer airports or outer pickup points to avoid central driving. Train-station pickup can work well if you arrive by rail and leave the city quickly.

What insurance should I choose?

Verify the excess and exclusions (tires, glass, undercarriage). Avoid duplicate coverage if your card or another policy already covers rentals.

How do I avoid surprise fees?

Confirm fuel policy, mileage limits, deposit amount, and cross-border rules. Photograph the car at pickup and return, and keep your return receipt.

Do I need an International Driving Permit (IDP)?

It depends on your license country. Check the requirement for your issuing country before traveling to France.
