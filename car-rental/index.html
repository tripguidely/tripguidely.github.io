Insurance basics (simple, practical)

Understand the levers before the counter pitch.

  • CDW/LDW: reduces your liability for damage/theft (often with an excess).
  • Excess/deductible: your out-of-pocket maximum if something happens.
  • Third-party liability: required in many places; know what’s included.
  • Credit card coverage: sometimes duplicates CDW but has rules (read the fine print).
  • Deposit hold: big holds happen with “basic” coverage—plan your card limit.
Rule of thumb: Compare offers using the same insurance level. “Cheap” can get expensive fast once you add coverage at the counter.

Pickup checklist (do this every time)

Prevents most disputes.

  1. Photo walkaround: film + photos (all sides + roof + wheels).
  2. Document existing damage: make sure it’s on the paperwork before leaving.
  3. Fuel policy: confirm “full-to-full” (or the exact rule).
  4. Mileage: verify unlimited vs capped distance.
  5. Tolls: confirm toll device rules + admin fees.
  6. Return plan: know the exact return location + after-hours process.
Common trap: leaving without the damage noted in writing. Photos help, but the contract note is stronger.

FAQ

Is it cheaper to rent at the airport or downtown?

It depends. Airports can have more inventory and competition, but also extra fees. Downtown can be convenient but may have limited hours and stricter return logistics. Use the city guide to pick the best pickup for your trip style.

Do I need full coverage?

Not always. The best choice depends on your risk tolerance, driving environment, and whether your credit card covers CDW. The important part is comparing total cost with the same coverage level.

What should I always do at pickup?

Do a photo/video walkaround and ensure any existing damage is written on the rental agreement before you leave the lot.
